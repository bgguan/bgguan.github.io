Top Five Favorite Movies

During the summer, many of us are tormented by the one thing we are supposed to be free from: boredom. Luckily, a good movie marathon is a great cure for this summertime weariness. Say goodbye to sitting around and doing nothing with my favorite films for you to watch!



#5 - La La Land

Rating: PG-13; Personal Age Rating: 10+

One of the most well known movies of 2016 was La La Land. Everyone argued about whether it was good or not. Some called it cliche, some said it was an amazing movie about everyday life in LA. It seems like everyone has an opinion about this film.

I loved it.

La La Land was a lovely film. The visuals looked like they were all tailor-made for Instagram, and the music was absolutely beautiful. Yes, some moments were boring, but it was all saved by colorful montages and catchy music. I mean, who wasn't singing "City of Stars" after the movie ended?

#4 - Wonder

Rating: PG; Personal Age Rating: 8+

Wonder. An amazing book that turned into an amazing movie. When I first read the books, and I found out about the movie, I admit, I was skeptical. How were they going to change this moving book into an equally emotional movie? Well, for the most part, they succeeded.

The movie was extremely well crafted, with some extraordinary acting. Jacob Tremblay, the actor who played Auggie, portrayed all the emotions required for the movie very well for his age. Julia Roberts, Auggie's mom, was also an great actress. You could tell the whole film was made passionately and that everyone was truly invested in the project. If you're looking for a good, touching film, watch Wonder.

#3 - The Greatest Showman

Rating: PG; Personal Age Rating: 9+

The Greatest Showman was a film that got a lot of criticism. If you want proof, you just have to look at its 56% Rotten Tomatoes rating. However, just because it wasn't loved by critics doesn't mean that it's really bad. The movie is an explosion of interesting visuals and great music.

The film is truly fantastic, with the lyric writers from La La Land (Benj Pasek and Justin Paul) doing the songs. The performances was great, too, with Hugh Jackman and Zendaya. Though many historical innacuracies are present, if you watch the movie just looking for a fun flick, you may just find what you are looking for.

#2 - A Wrinkle in Time

Rating: PG; Personal Age Rating: 8+

I admit, when I first saw the trailer for this movie, I wasn't completely interested. I mean, the bookA Wrinkle in Time was one of my favorite books of all time. Madeleine L'Engle was a great writer, and she really made the story unique for me. How would they transform an excellent story into a good movie?

From its rating on here, you can tell that I loved it.

I watched the movie two times in theaters using Moviepass. The movie was visually stunning, with graphics that would awe even the most avid CGI-hater. Though it may have changed some parts of the story, A Wrinkle in Time managed to, for the most part, keep a core element of the original book, love. After all, it is what allows Meg to save Charles Wallace in the end.

In the end, A Wrinkle in Time isn't perfect. But in end, it showed strong messages of self-love and loyalty. Plus, it doesn't hurt that the visuals were beautiful as well.
